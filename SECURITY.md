# **Responsible Disclosure Policy (Vulnerability Disclosure)**

## **Introduction**

Sentinel Security Labs, LLC ("Sentinel") is committed to maintaining the integrity and security of our infrastructure and the data entrusted to us by our partners. We recognize the vital role that independent security researchers play in the global security ecosystem and welcome reports regarding potential vulnerabilities.

## **Safe Harbor**

Any security research conducted in good faith and in accordance with this policy will be considered "authorized" conduct. Sentinel will not initiate legal action against researchers who:

* Provide a detailed description of the vulnerability.  
* Provide Sentinel a reasonable amount of time to resolve the issue before any public disclosure.  
* Do not access or modify data that does not belong to them.  
* Do not perform any testing that would degrade the quality of our services (e.g., Denial of Service).

## **Reporting a Vulnerability**

If you believe you have discovered a security vulnerability, please submit a report to our technical directorate immediately.

* **Primary Technical Contact:** cto@getsentinel.org  
* **Corporate Oversight:** michael@getsentinel.org

**Please include the following in your report:**

1. **Description:** A detailed summary of the vulnerability.  
2. **Impact:** The potential risk associated with the flaw.  
3. **Steps to Reproduce:** Proof of Concept (PoC) scripts, screenshots, or step-by-step instructions.  
4. **Environment:** Details on the browser, OS, or specific endpoint where the flaw was found.

## **Scope**

This policy applies to the following domains:

* \*.getsentinel.org  
* \*.getsentinellabs.com

## **Exclusions & Prohibited Conduct**

The following activities are strictly prohibited and fall outside the scope of Safe Harbor:

* **Denial of Service (DoS/DDoS):** Any testing that impacts the availability of services.  
* **Social Engineering:** Phishing or physical security testing of Sentinel staff or partner school district personnel.  
* **Data Exfiltration:** Accessing, downloading, or sharing sensitive partner data (e.g., PII or district credentials).  
* **Third-Party Services:** Vulnerabilities in third-party providers (e.g., Oracle Cloud, Google Workspace) should be reported directly to those entities.

## **Our Commitment**

Upon receipt of a valid report, Sentinel will:

* Acknowledge receipt of your report within **48 business hours**.  
* Provide an estimated timeline for remediation.  
* Notify the researcher once the vulnerability has been resolved.

*“Securing the human element requires a collaborative defense.”*
